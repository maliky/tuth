**** Student Uploads Registration Documents
:PROPERTIES:
:PRIMARY_ACTOR: Student
:SCOPE: Registration Module
:LEVEL: User-goal level
:END:
***** Preconditions
- Student logged into Tuth system.
- Registration period open.

***** Success Guarantee
- Documents uploaded and pending verification by the Enrollment Department.

***** Main Success Scenario
1. Student logs into the registration interface.
2. System verifies student's identity.
3. Student selects "Upload Documents" action.
4. Student selects required document files (e.g., ID, transcripts).
5. Student confirms the upload.
6. System validates the file types and sizes.
7. System stores documents securely and updates student's status to "pending verification".
8. System confirms successful upload.

***** Extensions (Alternative scenarios)
- 6a. Invalid document type or size:
  1. System alerts the student to the specific error.
  2. Student corrects and re-uploads documents.

- 7a. System fails during document storage:
  1. System notifies the student of an internal error.
  2. Student retries upload or contacts IT support.

***** Postconditions
- Documents available for Enrollment Department's verification.
- Student status updated to indicate document submission status.

**** Student Checks Financial and Enrollment Status
:PROPERTIES:
:PRIMARY_ACTOR: Student
:
SCOPE: Student Dashboard
:LEVEL: User-goal level
:END:
***** Preconditions
- Student logged into Tuth system.

***** Success Guarantee
- Student clearly views accurate and current financial and enrollment status.

***** Main Success Scenario
1. Student logs into their student dashboard.
2. System verifies student's identity and displays dashboard.
3. Student navigates to "Financial & Enrollment Status" section.
4. System retrieves student's financial clearance and enrollment data.
5. System displays current balance, payment deadlines, and enrollment conditions (e.g., fully enrolled, partially enrolled due to incomplete payments/documents).

***** Extensions (Alternative scenarios)
- 4a. System unable to retrieve data due to internal issues:
  1. System informs student of temporary data retrieval issue.
  2. Student contacts support or retries later.

- 5a. Student sees "partially enrolled" status due to financial issues:
  1. Student reviews details of pending financial requirements.
  2. Student initiates contact with Financial Officer for clarification or resolution.

***** Postconditions
- Student aware of their current academic and financial standing.
- Information displayed guides the student's next steps clearly.

    Merit-based- aid is a form of college financial aid that does not consider a student's financial need, but rather is awarded based on an outstanding academic performance, leadership and or have been admitted to a particular academic discipline

    Financial Aid- Financial aid is any college funding that doesn't come from family or personal savings or earnings. It can take the form of grants, scholarships, work-study.

    Need-based- scholarships and grants are exactly what their title indicates: money for students with a financial need and no academic performance is attached before been awarded

    Land Grants- scholarship awarded to recommended and selected individuals from their local authorities base on an agreement through an Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between two institutions for the sole purpose of providing higher education for her citizens from the selected communities

The Office of the Registrar at Tubman University is dedicated to providing exceptional service to students, faculty, staff, and alumni. Our office manages academic records, course registration, graduation services, and policies that ensure the integrity of the university's academic mission.
Our Services

    Course Registration & Enrollment

    Our team supports students throughout the registration process, helping them manage course schedules, add or drop classes, and navigate the university’s registration system.
    Academic Records

    The Registrar’s Office maintains all academic records, ensuring accuracy and privacy. Students can request official transcripts, view grades, and update personal information through our secure online portal.
    Graduation Services

    We guide students through the graduation process, from applying for graduation to ensuring that all degree requirements are met.
    Verification & Certification

    We provide enrollment verifications, degree certifications, and respond to third-party verification requests for student status and academic standing.
    Transfer Credit Evaluation

    The Registrar evaluates transfer credits to ensure they align with university standards, helping students receive appropriate credit for their previous academic work.

Registration Process

    Pre-Registration Preparation

        Academic Advising: Before registering, students are required to meet with their academic advisors to discuss their course selections for the upcoming semester. This ensures that they are on track to meet their degree requirements and maintain a balanced academic workload.

        Clearing Holds: Students should ensure there are no holds on their accounts (such as financial, academic, readmission, or disciplinary holds) that could prevent them from registering. Holds can be cleared by contacting the relevant offices.
    Course Selection

        Course Offerings: Review the course catalog or the online registration system to explore the available courses for the semester. The catalog includes information on course codes, schedules, credit hours, and prerequisites.

        Creating a Schedule: After consulting with your advisor, create a schedule that balances core and elective courses. Be mindful of time conflicts and make sure to account for all required courses.
    Online Registration

        Login: Access the university’s online student portal using your student credentials.

        Select Courses: Use the course search function to find the courses discussed during advising. Add them to your registration cart.

        Register for Classes: Once your course selection is complete, proceed to officially register. The system will prompt you to confirm your choices.

        Drop/Add Period: Usually occurs in the second week instruction. This is to allow students to adjust their course schedules according to their wish.
    Confirmation of Enrollment

        Enrollment Verification: After successful registration and payment, confirm your enrollment through the student portal. You will receive an official confirmation of your registered courses and payment status.
    Late Registration and Withdrawal

        Late Registration: If you miss the regular registration period, you may still register during the late registration period, though late fees may apply.

        Withdrawal from Courses: a students may withdraw from a course after the add/drop period, but they should be aware of the withdrawal deadlines (before midterm)

Important Dates to Remember

    Registration Open Dates:The registration window opens during registration and classes when registration is closed. Check the academic calendar for these dates.

**Summary of key choices:**
- Object-level permissions (django-guardian) address most permission checks.
- Admin customization (`save_model`, `get_readonly_fields`, and `get_form`) controls specific editing constraints.
- Field validation (model-level choices and validation) is ideal for fixed-choice constraints.
- Admin inlines are ideal for related object visibility.

  
** Use Case Constraints and Recommended Approaches

*** (1) Adding prerequisites to a course
- =Roles=: chair, dean, vpaa
- =Restriction=: Only for courses within user's college.
- =Approach=:
  1. Use object-level permissions with django-guardian.
  2. Assign "change_course" or a custom permission (e.g., "add_prerequisite") at the Course level per college.
  3. Limit admin interface access to prerequisites via inlines with object-permission checks.

*** (2) Dean imports courses from CSV (prerequisites included)
- =Role=: dean
- =Restriction=: Only courses linked to a curriculum within dean's own college.
- =Approach=:
  1. Use Django's import-export.
  2. Customize `CurriculumAdmin` import method to:
     - Check the user's role (Dean).
     - Validate imported curriculum's college against dean's college.
     - Reject imports violating this constraint with clear error messages.

*** (3) Dean restricted to add curriculum only within own college
- =Role=: dean
- =Restriction=: Can't create curriculum outside their college.
- =Approach=:
  1. Override `CurriculumAdmin.get_form()` to:
     - Auto-set and lock "college" field based on dean’s college.
     - Or use form validation to reject invalid college selection.
  2. Limit the `college` queryset in the admin form dynamically.

*** (4) Chair can edit curriculum only if inactive
- =Role=: chair
- =Restriction=: Edit curriculum only if `is_active=False`.
- =Approach=:
  1. Use `CurriculumAdmin.get_readonly_fields()` to dynamically set all fields as read-only if curriculum is active for chairs.

     ```python
     def has_change_permission(self, request, obj=None):
         if request.user.groups.filter(name="Chair").exists() and obj and obj.is_active:
             return False

     ```
