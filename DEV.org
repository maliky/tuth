* Academic Dashboard Plan

** Summary

Build dean and chair dashboards around the current semester, giving academic leaders
a fast view of student enrollment by program, faculty workload, and schedule
coverage. Use active/approved curricula as the main view, while keeping inactive or
pending curricula visible but secondary. Curriculum detail pages should show courses
grouped by level and semester, with credit summaries and prerequisite graph output.

** Implementation Status

*** DONE P1 Dean dashboard program enrollment chart

- The dean dashboard shows active approved programs for the dean's college with
  proportional horizontal stacked bars by student level.
- Program rows preserve active programs with zero registrations and suppress the
  level legend when the scoped chart has no enrollment data.

*** DONE P2 Semester selector for academic dashboards

- Dean and chair dashboard contexts expose a dashboard term selector.
- The selected semester is used for enrollment, workload, and schedule summaries.

*** DONE P3 Dean chair drilldown selector

- The dean dashboard exposes a chair selector for chairs in the dean's college.
- Selecting a chair adds a focused chair dashboard context below the dean summary.

*** DONE P4 Chair dashboard v1 scope

- Chair scope uses active role assignment department or college when present.
- Department remains an optional grouping layer, with faculty profile department
  and college fallbacks.

*** DONE P5 Faculty workload and schedule summaries

- Academic dashboards include program workload rows with faculty count, section
  count, and credit totals.
- Academic dashboards include compact schedule placement rows for the selected
  semester.

*** DONE P6 Curriculum list official-first grouping

- The dean curriculum page shows approved active curricula first.
- Inactive, pending, historical, or unapproved curricula are shown in a collapsed
  secondary section.

*** DONE P7 Curriculum detail course grouping

- Curriculum details group programmed courses by level and semester.
- The page shows credit totals per level group, semester, year, and curriculum.

*** DONE P8 Read-only prerequisite graph display

- Missing graph exports are reported without generating new graph files.

** Dashboard Requirements

*** Dean dashboard

- Show enrollment statistics for the dean's college, grouped by program and student
  level: freshman, sophomore, junior, senior, and other configured levels.
- Use proportional stacked bars as the default visualization: each program is one
  bar, total bar length represents enrollment count, segments represent levels, and
  segment styling or tooltip includes gender split.
- Show faculty and workload summaries for programs in the college: faculty count,
  assigned sections, credit hours taught, and overload flags when available.
- Provide access to chair-level views through a selector so the dean can inspect a
  specific chair's dashboard context.
- Keep course offerings and timetable views available through the chair-style
  drilldown instead of duplicating all chair detail on the dean landing page.

*** Chair dashboard

- A chair can cover one or more responsibility scopes; department is optional, not
  the only organizing unit.
- V1 should use existing role assignment scope where possible, with department as an
  optional grouping category.
- Show program statistics like the dean view, scoped to the chair's programs and
  courses.
- Show current-semester timetable, room placement, faculty assignments, and student
  section coverage.
- Provide a semester dropdown so the chair can switch the dashboard from current
  semester to another semester.

** Curriculum Requirements

*** Curriculum list

- First show active and approved curricula.
- Then show inactive, pending, historical, or unapproved curricula in a collapsed
  section.
- Within each section, approved curricula come before pending or revision curricula.

*** Curriculum detail

- Group courses by level, then by semester.
- Show credit totals per semester, per year, and for the full curriculum.
- Below the course listing, show the prerequisite graph generated from the existing
  prerequisite graph export logic.
- Make dirty or imported curricula visibly distinct from approved active curricula.

** Assumptions

- Use proportional stacked bars, not scaled pies, for v1 because they compare
  program size, level mix, and gender split more reliably.
- Use existing =RoleAssignment=, department, and faculty relationships for chair
  scope in v1; avoid adding a new chair-program mapping model until the
  responsibility model is confirmed.
- Keep prerequisite graph rendering read-only on curriculum detail pages; do not
  change graph generation behavior in this dashboard pass.
